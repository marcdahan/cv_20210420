How to Lead Digital Disruption With Programmable Infrastructure

Published 17 February 2020 - ID G00466287 - 12 min read

By Hassan Ennaciri, Chris Saunderson
The pace and scope of digital transformation requires cloud-enabled IT infrastructure that scales to meet rapidly changing business needs. I&O leaders must adopt programmable infrastructure to enhance customer-focused agility using DevOps practices.

Overview

Key Challenges

  • I&O teams that rely on manual processes and legacy platforms will fail to meet infrastructure delivery demands.
  • Traditional ways of managing infrastructure inhibits I&O teams from taking advantage of the scalability that cloud provides.
  • I&O leaders supporting DevOps initiatives struggle to deliver programmable infrastructure (PI) due to lack of relevant skills.

Recommendations

I&O leaders responsible for delivering business value with DevOps should:
  • Deliver self-service, API-driven platforms by leveraging programmable infrastructure to enhance the agility of product teams.
  • Enhance business agility by delivering programmable infrastructure to enable cloud-native application development.
  • Increase the know-how of programmable infrastructure by developing software engineering skills within I&O teams and adopting communities of practice to institutionalize knowledge.

Strategic Planning Assumption

By 2022, creating a flexible, adaptive infrastructure will be among the top I&O goals for more than 70% of enterprises, which is a major increase from fewer than 20% in 2019.

Introduction

I&O leaders are under continuous pressure to adopt emerging, disruptive technologies and innovative approaches to keep pace with digital transformations. To drive infrastructure-led disruption (ILD) and rapidly deliver business value, many I&O leaders are implementing programmable infrastructure (PI). In the 2018 Gartner Programmable Infrastructure Survey, respondents indicated that the top four expected benefits of PI adoption were increased agility, fewer infrastructure failures, reduced cost and improved efficiency.
Unfortunately, many I&O teams lack the software engineering skills and collaborative structures needed to effectively leverage PI practices that enhance speed and agility. The Gartner PI Survey found that 61% of respondents indicated that the lack of staff expertise and skills is their top barrier to adopting programmable infrastructure.
I&O leaders who fail to provide agile, scalable infrastructure services will risk being excluded from digital transformation initiatives. Thus, I&O leaders are seeking strategies to enable their teams to successfully adopt PI platforms that are service-centric and enhance customer value. Analysis

Deliver Self-Service Platforms Using Programmable Infrastructure to Enhance Business Agility

PI requires software-defined infrastructure that I&O teams can manage and operate via application programming interfaces (APIs), automation and orchestration. While PI is easier to implement in public clouds, most organizations will need to maintain a hybrid infrastructure that includes on-premises data centers, edge computing and public clouds. The need for hybrid IT leads to a natural synergy between data center modernization and public cloud adoption. By modernizing to software-defined compute, storage and network, I&O teams can implement PI with API access to services (see â€œFrom APIs to Ecosystems: API Economy Best Practices for Building a Digital Platformâ€). This transformation enables complete automation and adoption of infrastructure as code.
I&O leaders must acquire software-defined platforms to drive hardware refresh and replacement for on-premises infrastructure. These platforms must be manageable and configurable via APIs. By selecting hardware platforms based on automation requirements, I&O leaders can build a continuous integration/continuous delivery (CI/CD) pipeline as a part of their PI delivery approach. Existing investments in infrastructure automation tools such as Ansible, Chef, Puppet and SaltStack can be reused to integrate with the CI/CD pipeline to make PI adoption more seamless.
By using programmable infrastructure, I&O leaders can enhance their role as infrastructure services orchestrators and provide self-service platforms that evolve with the needs of consumers. I&O teams can assist developers in migrating appropriate workloads to public clouds and take advantage of scalability, elasticity and other advanced features available in cloud-native technologies. These technologies include serverless, container platforms and analytics platforms.
I&O teams can help developers run workloads on-premises when regulatory or other requirements demand this approach. Since PI delivers I&O services by exposing predefined, secure APIs, development teams will be able to consume services on-demand. An API-driven approach enables developers to access secure compliant services as quickly as needed without having to hand off provisioning requests or understand the details of the underlying infrastructure.

As I&O teams evolve their PI implementations, they must embed agile practices to develop, test and release infrastructure code. They should use DevOps practices leveraging a CI/CD toolchain, storing code in code repositories, version controlling, testing, using release management, and maintaining a commitment to quality and reliability.
As their adoption of PI becomes more mature, I&O teams will be able to release more advanced services in a structured fashion. These services could include:
  • Site reliability engineering (SRE) practices (see â€œDevOps Teams Must Use Site Reliability Engineering to Maximize Customer Valueâ€)
  • The introduction of chaos engineering (see â€œHow to Safely Begin Chaos Engineering to Improve Reliabilityâ€)
  • Advanced analytics to improve visibility, reliability and performance

Deliver Programmable Infrastructure to Enable Cloud-Native Application Development

The delivery demands placed on I&O organizations require agile, scalable and resilient infrastructure solutions. PI enables I&O leaders to deliver services that fulfill customer needs, while also maturing, evolving and innovating based on stakeholder feedback (see â€œProgrammable Infrastructure Is Foundational to Infrastructure-Led Disruptionâ€). Building on this foundation enhances the value I&O can demonstrate, highlighting the positive nature of ILD (see â€œThe Future of I&O Is Infrastructure-Led Disruptionâ€).
One of the greatest barriers to scaling DevOps initiatives is manual environment provisioning. A lack of automated provisioning will constrain the flow of value from development to production. In many cases, the number of manual handoffs required will cause considerable delays and impact the quality and reliability of the software releases.
Our research demonstrates that I&O teams have an opportunity to transform the way they operate their infrastructure service and increase their participation in value delivery. An increasing number of I&O leaders are using platform teams to deliver infrastructure services as an agile product. This approach provides organizations with an efficient technique to fulfill customer needs and accommodate I&O requirements. Building a shared platform team takes advantage of economies of scale and expertise within I&O teams to deliver services through API interfaces or as a self-service platform. The platform can serve multiple DevOps and product teams. Like any agile software product, the platform has its own life cycle to meet the demands of its consumers. As organizations mature and scale PI practices, I&O leaders should use platform teams as their main infrastructure service delivery method (see Figure 3 and â€œHow to Scale DevOps by Building Platform Teamsâ€).
Figure 3. Leverage PI to Deliver I&O Services to Development Teams
The self-service platform is a logical point to introduce PI to development teams and incorporate it into their delivery pipelines. This approach enables development teams to adopt modern, cloud-native applications without having to learn the underlying platform technologies. Public cloud providers are continuously releasing new services, and new technologies are emerging (such as machine learning, advanced analytics, Kubernetes and serverless). I&O leaders must curate those services and make them available for developers to consume effectively and securely from the platform. In doing so, developers can focus exclusively on functional product requirements. This accelerates the delivery of value from the idea stage to running in production.
I&O leaders will build the foundation for ILD by implementing PI and other advanced practices to deliver modern, scalable and resilient infrastructure platforms. Leveraging PI to deliver platforms empowers I&O teams to be proactive and a major contributor to the success of digital transformations. This can deliver three key business benefits:
  1. Speed â€” By providing developers and product teams with on-demand access to I&O services, I&O leaders will enhance agility and eliminate handoffs and wait states. Based on Gartner client inquiries and interactions, timely environment provisioning is a considerable constraint to delivering software to production.
  2. Reliability and availability â€” I&O leaders who build services using PI will ensure greater resiliency to failures and can leverage cloud-native application architectures to achieve autoscaling and autorecovery. PI enables DevOps teams to take advantage of cloud provider resiliency innovations, such as availability zones and global reach. These features maximize service availability and expedite system recovery in case of a disaster.
  3. Visibility â€” PI services are granular, providing broader visibility into the performance, configuration and stability of services and their underlying infrastructure. Consumers of the platforms, including business stakeholders, can also view dashboards with meaningful metrics to help them measure product value (including cost) and success. Stakeholders can analyze the telemetry and usage analytics to evaluate the product ROI and to compare outcomes to previous versions or feature releases. In addition, the shared visibility of logs and metrics across teams will foster greater trust and collaboration.

Develop PI Software Engineering Skills and Build Communities of Practice to Institutionalize Knowledge

PI provides I&O leaders with a unique approach for provisioning and managing their infrastructure. With PI, I&O teams leverage software engineering techniques to eliminate manual processes used to operate and deliver services. While this automated approach is promising, I&O leaders are struggling to develop adequate PI knowledge and software engineering skills within their I&O teams. As a result, they are often unable to meet digital business demands for rapid, scalable change.
To ensure organizational readiness for PI adoption, I&O leaders must:
  1. Identify initial areas in IT to employ PI. Start with an appropriate pilot and a carefully selected team to promote experimentation and rapid learning. Then, clearly set goals and metrics to demonstrate success in PI delivery, including measures that are relevant to business stakeholders, to build broad support for adoption.
  2. Develop a list of required skills for PI adoption. I&O leaders must partner with other teams, such as development and security, to identify the specific skills needed to implement PI. They must also identify potential sources for these required skills outside of I&O. This activity not only enables I&O leaders to pinpoint strengths and weaknesses across the organizationâ€™s current skills portfolio, but it also lays the foundation of collaboration needed for successful PI adoption.   1. Set up communities of practice. I&O teams must embrace a culture of collaboration and continuous learning to effectively leverage partnerships with developers and drive PI adoption. I&O leaders should encourage continuous learning by providing their teams with supported access to educational resources both inside and outside of the organization. In particular, they will greatly benefit from setting up a community of practice (COP) focused on PI. This COP will help develop skills and competencies, enabling cross-functional teams to share knowledge, socialize and communicate forward-looking information about each domain represented in the community. As the COP matures, I&O leaders should start including additional domains in the community, such as security and governance, to improve and adapt their current practices. This continuous learning forum will help consolidate knowledge and share best practices to accelerate skills development and drive innovation (see â€œFoster Communities of Practice to Ensure Successful DevOpsâ€).
